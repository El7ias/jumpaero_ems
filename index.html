THE PROBLEM

A Response Time Crisis

Across America, millions live too far from life-saving emergency care. In rural communities, the difference between survival and tragedy is a clock no one can stop.

14min+

Median Rural Response

Median rural emergency response times exceed 14 minutes — double the life-saving threshold. For 10% of rural calls, delays approach 30 minutes.

7-10%

Survival Loss Per Minute

For cardiac arrest, survival chances decrease 7-10% per minute without intervention. Every second truly counts.

60%

Rural Trauma Deaths

Rural residents account for 60% of trauma-related deaths despite making up only 15-20% of the population.

60M+

Rural Americans Underserved

Over 60 million Americans live in rural areas with limited or no access to advanced emergency medical services.

THE PROCESS

From 911 Call to On-Scene Care

A radically faster way to get a trained paramedic to the people who need help most.

01

Emergency Dispatched

A 911 call is received. The system identifies an emergency suited for rapid aerial response — cardiac events, trauma, stroke, or situations where ground transport faces critical delays.

02

60-Second Launch

A pilot-paramedic receives the alert, boards the aircraft, and is airborne in under 60 seconds. Vertical takeoff means no runway needed — launch from a station pad, hospital roof, or prepared site.

03

High-Speed Transit

The aircraft transitions to high-speed cruise at up to 288 mph — covering a 30-mile radius in under 8 minutes. No traffic, no road conditions, no detours. Direct line to the patient.

04

On-Scene Care Begins

Vertical landing near the patient — driveways, fields, parking lots, hillsides up to 10° slopes. The paramedic begins advanced life support immediately, stabilizing the patient for ground ambulance transport.
